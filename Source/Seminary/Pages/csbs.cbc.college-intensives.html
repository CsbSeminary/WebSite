2014 Seminary Summer Courses

Bonhoeffer in His Context (2T2233)

2 Credit Hours (on campus)

Instructor: Dr. Jimmy Cobb

Course Description: Just a few clarifications about the Bonhoeffer course: (1) We will deal with Bonhoeffer's theology, listen to his own words with minimum input from his various interpreters. (2) Pre-work reading assignment for the first day of class in order to cover his main material. The textbook, The Bonhoeffer Reader offers students a chance to read his own words from different phases of his life. We will certainly review his better known writings (Cost of Discipleship and Life Together) but also consider his lesser known writings. We will concentrate on his writings in the mornings. (3) In the afternoons, we'll lighten up a bit with video material mainly about his life and times which hopefully will help students understand his theology in his life context.

Dates: May 5-9, 2014

Times: MON 9:00am-4:00pm, TUE 9:00am-4:00pm, WED 9:00am-4:00pm, THU 9:00am-4:00pm, FRI 9:00am-4:00pm

Effective Ministry Management (2E1211)

2 Credit Hours (on campus) 

Instructor: Dr. Rob Blackaby & Mr. Peter Blackaby

Course Description: This course examines how to address planning and administration needs within an established church, a church start, or Christian ministry. Emphasis will be given to planning, organizing, providing resources, establishing administrative guidelines, and developing skills in evaluation for continued ministry growth and effectiveness.
