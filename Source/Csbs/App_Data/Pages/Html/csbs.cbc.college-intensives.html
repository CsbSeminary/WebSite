Evangelism & Church Planting with a European Worldview

2 Credit Hours (on campus)

Instructor: Dr. Mark Edworthy (IMB)
Dr. Mark Edworthy is a top leader at the International Mission Board of the SBC giving strategic direction to the work among the European people groups. He was appointed as a church planter to Poland in 1991 and planted the first IMB-initiated church in the former Soviet Union. It was in those early days that he and his wife Suzie became good friends to  the Booths when they were serving in Hungary. Mark comes with a broad range of practical ministry experience (at one time he even coached an American football team in Poland as an outreach strategy); and he is a great teacher and public speaker. He has written one of the books that will be used in the class entitled, “The Wall that Remains,” which chronicles some of the stories in Europe in those pivotal years after the fall of the Iron Curtain.

Course Description: Europe is not as monolithic in its worldview as it is may have been in the past. The current context includes Roman Catholics, Eastern Orthodox, Muslims, as well as post-modern atheists, not unlike the pluralistic setting of Canada in the 21st century. This course will address the challenges of sharing Christ in this context and will include Skyping with some who are currently doing just that. Students will learn principles of evangelism in cross-cultural settings as well as strategies for church planting. The instructor will also share about the history of Southern Baptist work in Europe among these peoples and discuss lessons learned.

Dates: May 6–10, 2013

2P3231 Spiritual Leadership

2 Credit Hours (on campus)

Instructors: Dr. Rob Blackaby
President & Professor of Ethics. B.A. in History and English, University of British Columbia; M.Div., Ph.D., Southwestern Baptist Theological Seminary. Dr. Blackaby has served as the senior pastor for twelve years in Trinity Baptist Church, Calgary, Alberta, and as the lead pastor for the church plant, Southwinds Church. He is assisting with the development of the SouthTrails church-planting network.  Dr. Blackaby served as president of the Canadian Convention of Southern Baptists from 2003-2006. He joined CSBS&C in 2007.

Dr. Richard Blackaby
Chancellor. B.A., University of Saskatchewan; M.Div. and Ph.D., Southwestern Baptist Theological Seminary. Dr. Blackaby pastored in Winnipeg, Manitoba before becoming President of Canadian Southern Baptist Seminary & College in 1993. He has co-authored 9 books with his father, Henry Blackaby including Spiritual Leadership: Moving People Onto God’s Agenda. Dr. Blackaby also served as managing editor and co-author with his father and brothers for The Blackaby Study Bible. His other published works include Putting a Face on Grace: Living a Life Worth Passing On. Dr. Blackaby is currently President of Blackaby Ministries International and keeps an active writing and speaking schedule. He began his role as Chancellor of CSBS&C in 2006.

Course Description: This course introduces students to the major concepts and resources for spiritual leadership and assists them in examining their current leadership effectiveness.

2E2221 Effective Bible Teaching

2 Credit Hours (on campus)

Instructor: Dr. Norma Hedin
Affiliated Professor. Norma Hedin is a graduate of William Jennings Bryan College (Dayton, TN) where she earned a BA in Christian Education, with a minor in Biblical Studies. She also earned a Master of Arts in Christian Education and PhD in Foundations of Education from Southwestern Baptist Theological Seminary (Fort Worth, TX). She has done post-doctoral work in Higher Education Administration at the University of North Texas.

 
Prior to her appointment to BHCTI, she was Professor of Foundations of Education at Southwestern from1990 to 2006. Dr. Hedin served as Associate Dean of Masters Degree programs from 1999-2004. She was recognized by “Who’s Who Among America’s Teachers” for eight years. She currently teaches and serves as a research consultant at Dallas Baptist University and teaches regularly at the Canadian Baptist Theological Seminary in Cochrane, Alberta.
 
Dr. Hedin has served on a church staff in educational ministry, and has conducted Christian education training seminars in teaching and learning. She is an active member of the North American Professors of Christian Education, where she has served on the Board of Directors and held elected offices. She currently serves as an area book review editor for the NAPCE publication, the Christian Education Journal. She has written chapters for textbooks, articles, and Bible study curriculum.
 
Dr. Hedin and her husband, Eric, have two daughters: Kirsten and Kayla.

Course Description: This course equips students to be effective teachers of God’s word so that people might be spiritually transformed. Emphasis will be given to the teacher's personal preparation, preparing a quality Bible study lesson, and using the time effectively in various Bible study settings and with different audiences.
